01

About

My research sits at the intersection of cognitive psychology and interaction design. I ask how people reason under uncertainty, how attention is distributed across digital environments, and how calm, legible design can reduce cognitive load rather than add to it.

Before joining Bergen, I completed my doctorate at KTH Royal Institute of Technology and held a postdoctoral fellowship at the Max Planck Institute for Human Development. I collaborate closely with designers, neuroscientists, and public-sector teams building tools for everyday use.

02

Research

  • Mental models of automation

    How users predict and trust automated systems, and what breaks that trust when behaviour becomes opaque.

  • Attention & interface calm

    Measuring the cognitive cost of visual noise and designing quieter, more legible information hierarchies.

  • Reasoning under uncertainty

    How probabilistic information should be represented so that people make better everyday decisions.
